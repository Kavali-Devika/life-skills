### 1. Activities that make me relax – Calm Quadrant

        - Reading a book for a few minutes helps me slow down mentally.

        - Spending time in nature makes me feel peaceful.

        - Writing my thoughts in a journal clears my mind.

        - Listening to devotional or instrumental music soothes me.

        - Drinking a warm cup of tea quietly feels comforting.

        - Practicing mindfulness meditation calms my thoughts.

        - Doing simple breathing exercises helps me regain balance.

### 2.When do I enter the Stress Quadrant? 
        - When I have unfinished work piling up.

        - When I compare myself to others.

        - When I receive unexpected responsibilities.

        - During exams or performance evaluations.

        - When I don’t understand a topic clearly.

        - When plans suddenly change without preparation.

### 3.How do I know I am in the Excitement Quadrant? 
        - I feel inspired to take action immediately.

        - My energy levels feel high and active.

        - I feel proud of my progress.

        - I look forward to challenges.

        - I feel mentally sharp and alert.

        - I naturally smile and feel happy while working.

### 4.Paraphrase of “Sleep is Your Superpower 

        - Proper sleep helps the brain store new information.

        - It repairs muscles and body tissues.

        - Sleep keeps emotions balanced and stable.

        - It strengthens the body’s defense system.

        - Quality sleep lowers the chance of serious health problems.

        - During sleep, the brain removes waste substances.

        - It increases concentration and decision-making ability.

        - Healthy sleep improves overall performance.

### 5.Ideas to Sleep Better  

        - Maintain a relaxing bedtime routine.

        - Avoid heavy meals before sleeping.

        - Limit late-night work or studying.

        - Keep electronic devices away from the bed.

        - Use comfortable bedding and pillows.

        - Listen to calm music before sleep.

        - Reduce noise and distractions in the room.

        - Go to bed only when feeling sleepy.

### 6.Brain-Changing Benefits of Exercise

        - Physical activity increases blood flow to the brain.

        - It improves problem-solving skills.

        - Exercise lowers mental fatigue.

        - It releases endorphins that create happiness.

        - It strengthens brain connections.

        - Regular workouts slow cognitive decline.

        - It improves confidence and self-esteem.

        - Exercise supports better emotional control.

### 7.Steps I Can Take to Exercise More 

        - Start with 10-minute daily workouts.

        - Follow online fitness videos at home.

        - Join a weekend fitness class.

        - Track daily steps using a mobile app.

        - Stretch during work breaks.

        - Cycle instead of using transport for short distances.

        - Reward myself for consistency.

        - Plan weekly physical activity goals.
